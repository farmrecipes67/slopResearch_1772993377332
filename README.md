## Overview

This repository hosts a research article that examines the concept of **"AI slop"** — the vast proliferation of low-quality, AI-generated content — through the historical lens of **incunabula**, the earliest books produced by movable type printing in the 15th century.

Just as the invention of the printing press led to an initial flood of hastily produced, often derivative printed material before standards, curation, and quality emerged, the current wave of generative AI content may represent a parallel transitional period in the history of information production. This research draws on that analogy to explore cultural, epistemological, and technological dimensions of AI-generated content in the modern information ecosystem.
